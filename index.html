McReynolds Family History

The McReynolds had their beginning in Allenton, Alabama in the early 1800s with a male slave named Anthony. Anthony was born in the West Indies in 1794 and spoke with a dialect. He had a dark complexion, was strong and had curly hair. He was a good carpenter and knew a great deal about farming, hunting and fishing. He was skilled with his hands and did good work. A white family with the surname of McReynolds brought Anthony to Allenton from Virginia for the purpose of breeding. During this era, slaves were given the last name of their masters, thus Anthony McReynolds.

After the signing of the Emancipation Proclamation in 1863, the white McReynolds family became uneasy about having slaves and sold their land and slaves in Allenton and the surrounding areas to the white Dale family and then moved away. In order to gain better favor with their masters, some of Anthony's children and grandchildren changed their names from McReynolds to Dale and moved to Monroe county where their new masters owned land.
