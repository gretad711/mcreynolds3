McReynolds Family Reunion

July 7, 2022 - July 10, 2022

Chicago, Illinois

Welcome McReynolds Family

Dear Family,

Words cannot express how delighted we feel that you are all here, together, smiling and catching up with each other today. It’s a feeling of sheer joy, seeing that spring has sprung and another few years have passed since we last met up like this. Although distance separates our family, the love we have for one another is evident by the presence of the 200 people that have gathered this weekend.

We only see each other once every few years; so let’s make it epic!  Let’s make it count! For those of you who didn’t think you would make it, and to all the rest of you, thank you for making the effort to get here, and make this event a special one for the rest of us.

Our wonderful committee has arranged a great weekend for you all. Our only wish for this reunion, is that all of you enjoy yourselves and walk away with some great, new memories.

Let us all rejoice together, old family and new family, and praise God for this opportunity he has provided to us so that we could be together on this joyous occasion.

May God continue to bless our family!

The Chicago McReynolds Family Reunion Committee

McReynolds Family History

The McReynolds had their beginning in Allenton, Alabama in the early 1800s with a male slave named Anthony. Anthony was born in the West Indies in 1794 and spoke with a dialect. He had a dark complexion, was strong and had curly hair. He was a good carpenter and knew a great deal about farming, hunting and fishing. He was skilled with his hands and did good work. A white family with the surname of McReynolds brought Anthony to Allenton from Virginia for the purpose of breeding. During this era, slaves were given the last name of their masters, thus Anthony McReynolds.

After the signing of the Emancipation Proclamation in 1863, the white McReynolds family became uneasy about having slaves and sold their land and slaves in Allenton and the surrounding areas to the white Dale family and then moved away. In order to gain better favor with their masters, some of Anthony's children and grandchildren changed their names from McReynolds to Dale and moved to Monroe county where their new masters owned land.
